# Why it exists
Added in peer review to close the document's main substantive gap. The agent-safety controls answer *can the agent act, and can we prove what it did?* They do not answer what a public body is **legally** accountable for: *may government lawfully take this decision, in this way, and can the person affected understand and challenge it?* Those duties bind regardless of MCP/A2A and largely cannot be delegated to a system — **for government the binding control is often law, not protocol.**

# What the law requires → where it lands
- **Automated decisions** — [DUAA 2025, Arts 22A–22D](../../uk-government/duaa-2025-adm.md) (in force 5 Feb 2026): for a significant solely-automated decision, give information, allow representations, enable human intervention/review, allow contest. → approval design; human-in-the-loop; a meaningful-human-involvement test.
- **DPIA / by design** — [UK GDPR Arts 35 & 25](../../uk-government/dpia-uk-gdpr.md). → mandatory pre-production gate.
- **Transparency** — [ATRS](../../uk-government/atrs.md), mandatory + spend-control gate. → registry/inventory.
- **Public-law duties** — lawfulness, rationality, procedural fairness, duty to give reasons; a **named human** stays the legal decision-maker. → identity records authority; evidence captures reasons.
- **Equality** — [PSED, Equality Act 2010 s.149](../../uk-government/equality-act-psed.md). → evaluation; bias testing.
- **Redress** — explanation, human review, appeal; judicial review. → incident response; governance.
